Char Dham Yatra

This is just an outline with the activities on a particular day, we can include more laid back leisure time or change the plan as you prefer.

Day 1

Haridwar - Barkot (205 Kms/5 - 6 hrs): After an early wakeup call morning drive to Barkot via Dehradun, Mussoorie, , Dhanaulti, Kanatal and Chamba . Kanatal Dhanaulti belt is also known as the fruit belt of the hill state of Uttarakhand, Later continue drive to Barkot. On arrival Barkot,check In at Hotel. Overnight stay at hotel.Barkot Also known as the king Shastrabahus Nagari, Barkot is a town at the bank of river Yamuna. First stop in the char dham route Barkot is famous for its nearness to kalpvriksh where one of the Saptarshi ashram was. Near by Gangnani is the place where gangas part flow and make a kund and thus it a holy place to visit to.

Day 2

Barkot - Yamunotri- Barkot (42 Kms & 5 Kms Trek) Each side:- after an early breakfast drive till Janki Chatti via Hanuman Chatti & Fool Chatti. Trek route from Janki Chatti to yamnotri is 06 Kms .In Yamnotri the ritual includes Holi Dip in Garam Kund, followed by Pooja and Darshan of Shree Yamunotri Ji. Post Darshan trek down towards Janki Chati followed by driving back to Barkot. Overnight stay at Hotel. Yamunotri :(Altitude 3,291 m)-- the western most shrine in the region, and to be visited first in order ,is dominated by peak of Banderpunch (Altitude 6,361 m) peak of Himalaya range .According to Hindu mythology Yamunotri is the daughter of Surya, the Sun and twin sister of Yama, the Lord of Death. The prevalent believe is whoever bathes in her waters will be spared a tortuous death. Hanuman Chatti :The confluence of Hanuman Ganga & Yamuna River. Surya Kund : The most important of the thermal springs in the vicinity of the temple is Surya kund which is symbol of Sun the father of Yamuna. Divya Shila :A rock pillar,Symbol of Yama ,the God of Death worshipped before entering the Yamunotri Temple.

Day 3

Barkot - Uttarkashi (95Kms, 4 Hours Approx.):- the day will start with a relax morning drive towards Uttarkashi which is approximately 82 kms from Barkot. Enroute visit Prakateshwar Cave. After we reached Uttarkashi the option to explore the local is open with many options to choose with. Overnight stay at the hotel. Uttarkashi : situated on the banks of river Bhagirathi ,Uttarkashiwhich means Kashi of north is situated at the bank of river Bhagiriathi . this is similar to the Varanashi in nature and has its significance in Char Dham route. Lies next to the Varun Parvat this place is home to manikarnika ghat and vishwanath temple. in the Centre of town this temple and Situated at the bank of river Bhagirathi the temple of Lord Vishwanath is located here where a massive iron trident is erected. The other important temples situated here are temple of Ekadash Rudra, Bhairav, Gyaneshwar and Goddess Kuteti Devi with varied religious and spiritual interest .

Day 4

Uttarkashi - Gangotri - Uttarkashi (100 Kms each way):- After an early breakfast drive to Gangotri via Bhatwadi, Harshil and Gangori. Entire stretch is filled with the sense of spirituality for which a devotee has travelled miles .Enroute Gangotri, Harshil - a small hemlet offering a magnificent view of the surrounding -is perhaps a real treat for the visitors. After reaching Gangotri and performance of rituals and Pooja relax for some time in the lovely surroundings. After a while in the afternoon drive back to Uttarkashi for night halt. on arrival check into the Hotel. Day 6 Uttarkashi ?tehri ?Rudraprayag -Syalsaud/Guptkashi( 235 kms) Early morning, drive to Guptkashi .Enroute visit Tehri Dam. Check in Hotel .night halt. Rudraprayag:One of the five Prayag in Uttarakhand is confluence of river Mandakini and river Alaknanda and named after Lord Rudra the Shiva . Temples : Rudranath and Chamunda Devi, Koteshwar Temple

Day 5

Uttarkashi- Guptkashi ( 250 kms) After an early breakfast drive to Guptkashi via tehri dam and Tilbada . perhaps the longest journey of road in Char Dham route

Day 6

Guptkashi -Bhimbali After an early Breakfast Drive up to Sonprayag and trek upto Bhimbali . Trek for 10 kms and make a camp stay there .Night halt there .

Day 7

Bhimbali - Kedarnath-Bhimbali (14kms Trek) After an early Breakfast 07 kms Trek start from Bhimbali After Darshan come back to Bhimbali . explore the temple area and surrounding . Evening Aarti and Morning Aarti would be performed on preannounced time. Kedarnath: one of the 12 Jyotirlingas and believed to be founded by Pandavas ,The Kedarnath shrine is one of the revered most temple in Hindu mythology. Present Kedarneth temple is believed to be built by Shri Shankaracharya in 7th century. Legend is after Mahabharata was over and knowing that they( pandavas) have committed sin by killing their own brothers , in order to absolve themselves of this sin, the Pandavas undertook a pilgrimage to take blessing from Lord Shiva. Lord Shiva was in Kailash in meditation and on learning that the Pandavas left Kashi Lord Shiva eluded. Pandavas were determine to take blessing from the Lord Shiva anyhow as only this would have absolve them from their committed sins?. Lord Shiva remains grounded in foothill of Himalaya and hence from then this placs is known as Guptkashi. Searching Lord Shiva from Basukedar, Pandavas went ahead till they reached Gaurikund in the kedarkhand of Himalaya. In Gurikund Nakul and Sahadev spotted a unique he-buffalo. Then Bheema went after the buffalo with his mace but could not catch it . But Bheema managed to hit the buffalo with his mace. The buffalo had its face hidden in a crevice-in the earth. Bheema started to pull it by its tail. In this tug-of war, the face of the buffalo went straight to Nepal, leaving its hind part in Kedar. On this place , a glorious JyotirLinga appeared and Lord Shiva appeared from this great light.. The pandavas were absolved of their sins when they get the Darshan of Lord Shiva .Than Lord Shiva told the Pandavas, ?From now on, I will remain here as a triangular shaped JyotirLinga and by taking a Darshan of Kedarnath, devotees would attain piety?. The custom of massage with ghee is practices here following when Bheema was struck with remorse. He started to massage Lord Shiva;s body with ghee in repent. Water and Bel leaves are used for worship.

Day 8

Bhimbali - Guptkashi (14Kms Down Trek & 35kms/1hr by Road) trek down to Gaurikund at leisure. After reaching Gaurikund drive back to hotel in,Guptkashi. Night Halt in hotel .

Day 9

Guptkashi - Badrinath (215kms/9hr) Early morning Drive towards Badrinath via Rudraprayag or Chopta. Enroute we will come across Joshimath and other places of similar interest. Check in Hotel. In the evening visit temple to attend Aarti and for obtaining token for Darshan on next day. Overnight stay. Badrinath ; Situated at an elevation of 3,133 meters and guarded by either side by mountain range Nar and Narayana Badrinath is most celebrated pilgrimage of the four Dham of Hindu Religion. According to the hagwat Purana "since time immemorial There in Badrikashram the Personality of Godhead (Vishnu), in his incarnation as the sages Nara and Narayana, had been undergoing great penance for the welfare of all living entities." According to Skand Purana there is no shrine in heaven , on earth and in in hell like Bdrinath.? The area around Badrinath was also celebrated in Padama Purana as abounding in spiritual treasures Places of Interest near Badrinath Tapt Kund : on the footstep of Temple and at the bank of river Alaknanda a natural thermal springs, where it is customary to bathe before entering the Badrinath temple. Narad Kund : a recess in the river near tapt kund where Shri Shankaracharya believe to recover the Badrinath idol Brahama Kapal : A flat platform on the bank of river Alaknanda. Hindus perform propitiating rites for their deceased ancestors. Sheshnetra : a boulder at 1500mts distance having an impression of the legendary serpent, better known as the Sheshnags eye. Charanpaduka : amidst a beautiful meadow there is a boulder on which the impression of Lord Vishnu foot are. distance ?approximately 3 kms. Mata Murty Temple : Devoted to the mother of Sri Badrinathji. Other important temples include Sesh Netra Temple , Urvashi Temple and Charanpaduka. Mana Village : the last village on the border and famous for its beauty .inhabitants are Bhotias ,an Indo-Mongolian tribe, during winter the tribe leave village for niti another village on downhill. Vasundhara : at a distance from Mana a magnificent waterfall and a real treat for the eyes at this height . approx distance from temple -5kms Bhim Pul :believed to be placed by Bhim, a massive rock lies over saraswati river performing the task of a bridge on the other side of Mana village. Presenting a spectacular view of water and nature this a continuous reminder of unfold mysteries of nature. Vyas Gufa (cave) : the cave near Mana village where Ved Vyas believed to have the Mahabharata and theer pauranic commentaries.

Day 10

Badrinath - Joshimath - Rudraprayag/ Srinagar (160-190kms/6-7hr) Early morning after Darshan of Badri Vishal and visiting near by places drive back to Rudraprayag via Joshimath. The drive in most part of the time will be alongside river Alaknanda. Joshimath: The religious center, established by Adi Shankaracharya, which he called Jyotirmath, later came to be known as Joshimath. Among the sites of interest are the temples of Nav Durga and Narsingh. Apart from its obvious religious importance, Joshimath is known for its scenic beauty. Next day final drop at Rishikesh/Haridwar
